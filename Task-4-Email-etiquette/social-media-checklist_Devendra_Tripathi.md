# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Devendra Tripathi – 25MIM10010**

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|-----|----------------|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional** | Recruiters and faculty search your name online. First impressions matter digitally too. |
| 2 | **Use strong, unique passwords** | Prevents unauthorised access to your accounts. Use a password manager if needed. |
| 3 | **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** | Adds a second layer of security even if your password is compromised. |
| 4 | **Think before you post** | Once something is online, it is very difficult to fully delete. Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor saw this?" |
| 5 | **Report cyberbullying or harassment** | Use platform reporting tools. Do not engage with or retaliate against bullies online. |
| 6 | **Keep your privacy settings updated** | Regularly review who can see your posts, location, and personal information. |
| 7 | **Give credit when sharing others' work** | Sharing content without attribution is a form of plagiarism and disrespectful to creators. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't | Why It's Harmful |
|---|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Don't share your OTP, password, or UPI PIN** | No bank, app, or institution will ever ask for this. Sharing it gives strangers full access to your accounts. |
| 2 | **Don't post your location in real time** | Sharing live location publicly (e.g., "at the library now") can be a safety risk. |
| 3 | **Don't engage in or spread rumours** | Spreading unverified information online can cause serious harm and may have legal consequences. |
| 4 | **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers** | Unknown contacts may use your personal information for phishing, fraud, or impersonation. |
| 5 | **Don't use social media for academic dishonesty** | Sharing exam papers, answers, or plagiarised content online can lead to serious disciplinary action. |
| 6 | **Don't click on links from unknown senders** | Phishing links disguised as offers, prize notifications, or alerts are the most common way accounts get hacked. |
